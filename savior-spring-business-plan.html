Executive Summary

Savior Spring Water is a grassroots, founder-led spring water subscription service operating in the DC-Maryland-Virginia (DMV) corridor. Two founders currently source, bottle, and deliver five-gallon jugs of natural spring water directly to customers' doors. The business operates on a subscription model with a compelling incentive: order three or more jugs and receive a free water dispenser.

Despite genuine product quality, loyal customers, and documented testimonials, the business lacks the digital infrastructure — a dedicated website, branded social media, email list, and CRM — needed to scale beyond word-of-mouth. This proposal outlines how to build that infrastructure rapidly, establish a brand identity worthy of the product, and structure sustainable growth in one of America's most densely populated and health-conscious metro areas.

Business Structure

Current State

The business currently operates informally — sourcing is done by the two founders, delivery is personal, and all marketing occurs through their personal Instagram accounts. There is no business entity, no dedicated banking, and no systems for managing customers, orders, or recurring revenue.

⚠️
Liability Exposure

Operating as individuals (not an LLC) means personal assets are exposed to any business liability. This needs to change immediately.

⚠️
No Business Banking

Mixing personal and business finances makes tax filing difficult and prevents access to business credit lines or loans later.

Additional Revenue Streams

B2B Accounts (Offices, Studios, Churches)

Commercial clients typically order 6–12 jugs per delivery. A single gym or office can represent $80–200/month. 10 commercial accounts = $1,000–$2,000/month added revenue.

Dispenser Sales & Accessories

Sell dispensers to non-subscribers, branded bottles, water flavor enhancers (mineral drops, electrolytes), and seasonal bundles. Low-lift revenue with existing customer base.

Gifting & Corporate Orders

Gift a spring water subscription. Seasonal boxes. Corporate holiday orders. High margin, low friction for existing infrastructure.

Events & Pop-Ups

Serve at local markets, health expos, community events. Free brand exposure plus direct sales. First sip is the best marketing tool you have.

Your current growth comes entirely from referrals — which is powerful. Formalize it:

  • Share referral codes via text, email, and Instagram DM.
  • Feature top referrers publicly ("Our most generous water spreader this month is...").

CRM & Email Strategy

Every person who visits your website, orders water, or follows your Instagram is a potential lifelong subscriber. A CRM (Customer Relationship Management system) is how you turn visitors into customers and customers into advocates. This is the single highest-ROI investment you can make right now.

Recommended CRM Stack (Budget-Friendly)

Tool Function Cost Why It
Klaviyo Email marketing + SMS + CRM. Captures website signups, segments lists, sends automated flows. Free up to 500 contacts Built for subscription businesses. Best-in-class automation.
Stripe Payment processing + subscription billing. Auto-charge recurring customers. 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction Industry standard. Easy setup. Links to Klaviyo natively.
Google Sheets (transitional) Simple customer database until CRM is fully set up. Track name, email, phone, address, plan, next delivery. Free Low barrier. Start today. Migrate to Klaviyo within 30 days.
Squarespace or Shopify Website platform with built-in email capture, subscription management, and payment. $29–$79/mo All-in-one. Links to Klaviyo. Mobile-optimized.

Email Automation Flows (Set Once, Earn Forever)

1. Welcome Series (5 emails over 7 days)
  • Day 0: "Welcome to the family — here's your discount"
  • Day 1: "Where your water comes from" (source story)
  • Day 3: "How to set up your dispenser"
  • Day 5: "5 reasons spring water changes everything"
  • Day 7: "Refer a friend, earn $10 credit"
2. Win-Back Flow (for inactive subscribers)
  • 30 days inactive: "We miss you — here's 10% off"
  • 45 days inactive: "Is everything okay with your delivery?"
  • 60 days inactive: "Last chance — pause or cancel easily"
3. Post-Purchase Flow
  • Immediately after order: confirmation + dispenser setup guide
  • 3 days after delivery: "How's the water? Leave us a Google review"
  • Upsell: "Ready to upgrade to 3 jugs and get a free dispenser?"
4. Monthly Newsletter
  • "This Month at the Spring" — source update
  • Subscriber spotlight / testimonial feature
  • Seasonal promotions and delivery schedule updates
  • Health tips, hydration content

Lead Capture: Website + Popup Strategy

The website included in this proposal features two built-in CRM capture points:

  • Auto-popup after 4 seconds: Captures name, email, and phone in exchange for "first drop perks" — subscriber discounts, early access to delivery windows, and new product announcements.
  • Dedicated email signup section: Mid-page and footer signup forms that feed directly into your email list.
  • Instagram bio link: Direct all Instagram traffic to the website, where the popup and signup form capture leads.
